This HIT will involve 3 short tasks, all involving pictures of turtles. It should take approximately 12-15 minutes to do all 3 tasks.

During the first task, you will see one turtle at a time and have to determine whether or not it is an aelonii. Aeloniis are a particular species of turtle. They are different from all other turtles in one simple way -- and your job is to find out what that is. For example, it could be that aeloniis all have 3 spots, but no other turtles do. Or aeloniis could be the only turtles that have a round shell; or green legs; or fins instead of legs.

As you can see from these examples, the turtles in this experiment will differ in a number of ways: (1) the number of spots, (2) the color of their spots, (3) the shape of their shell, (4) the shape of their head, (5) the shape of their legs, (6) the color of their body, and (7) the direction their tail is pointing

In the first task, you'll be shown a single turtle, and then be asked if it is or not an aelonii (press 'y' if yes, 'n' if no). Immediately after your response, you'll find out if you were right. At first, you'll have to guess. Eventually, though, you'll figure out what makes something an aelonii. You will have 0 turtles to judge. Do your best to determine what makes something an aelonii.
